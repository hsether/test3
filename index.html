Test 3: Rotator Cuff Tendon Repair on Humeral Head

This test matters because it's the exact kind of "looks simple, fails easily" medical render that separates real production-ready output from "medical-ish concept art."

What we're looking for is very specific, and very checkable:

  • Correct anatomy and vantage: Proximal humerus, humeral head, and a visible cut cancellous bone surface at the greater tuberosity
  • Correct construct: Two anchors seated in bone, two white pads, and two wide blue suture tapes crossing in an X to compress the tendon footprint
  • Clean studio composition: Minimal white background with soft translucent abstract wave shapes, no labels, no UI clutter
Show Prompt: Test 3, Rotator Cuff Repair ▼ 
High resolution, photorealistic 3D orthopedic medical illustration of a rotator cuff repair on the shoulder.

Show the proximal humerus with the rounded humeral head and a cut surface of cancellous bone at the greater tuberosity region, pale beige with porous texture. Surrounding is red striated muscle (deltoid and rotator cuff region) with realistic fiber direction and subtle sheen.

On the lateral aspect, depict a repaired tendon footprint secured with two small circular suture anchors seated in bone, each anchor head visible as a small beige or metallic disk. Two wide blue suture tapes cross over two smooth white protective pads or tendon compression devices, forming an X pattern that compresses the tendon down onto the bone footprint.

Composition is clean and centered, floating on a minimal white background with soft translucent abstract wave shapes behind the anatomy. Studio medical lighting, crisp focus, no text, no watermark, no logo.
